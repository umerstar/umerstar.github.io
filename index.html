I'm happiest when something I built is humming away on a shelf in my closet doing useful work. This site is where I'll post notes on what I'm running, building, and breaking.

What's running in my closet

A small homelab on Proxmox. The goal: self-host the things I'd otherwise rent from someone else, and learn the unglamorous parts of operations along the way.

  • A handful of LXC containers and VMs for services I rely on day-to-day.
  • Networking that's a bit more deliberate than the average home setup — VLANs, DNS I actually own, monitoring I can read.
  • n8n for the automations that quietly remove small annoyances from my life.

Hardware I poke at

ESP32 / Arduino

Sensors, telemetry, the occasional "why is there a microcontroller in my coffee setup."

Raspberry Pi

Bridges between the digital and physical — DIY tools, edge logging, home automation glue.

Drones (past life)

Quadcopter flight controllers, fiducial tracking. The hardware mindset still shapes how I think about software.

Networking gear

Switches, APs, a router that does more than its UI lets on. The invisible-when-it-works stuff.

Web work

By day: Next.js, Astro, TypeScript, deployed on Vercel. Marketing and product sites where performance and clarity matter more than framework flex.

What I value

  • Open source. I default to it when there's a choice, and I'm trying to give back more than I take.
  • Owning the stack. If I can run it myself, I usually will.
  • Being early. New protocols, new tools, new hardware — I'll try it.
  • Security and privacy. Both as a discipline and as a personal default.
  • Learning. I'd rather be a beginner at something interesting than an expert at something boring.
